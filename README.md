# Felicia Joy

Behavioral Science PhD Candidate — University of Chicago Booth School of Business

I study how organizations communicate, adopt new behaviors, and integrate new systems and technologies. My research combines behavioral science, natural language processing (NLP), and experimental methods to examine how communication, culture, and operational design shape organizational outcomes.

---

## Research Focus

**Conversational Receptiveness in Organizational Communication**

My dissertation examines how conversational receptiveness—linguistic signals that indicate openness to opposing viewpoints—shapes persuasion, trust, and stakeholder reactions when organizations communicate about complex or controversial issues.

**Organizational Culture as a Financial Asset**

I study workplace culture not only as a social construct but also as an operational and financial variable.

**Human–AI Operational Systems**

Organizations are beginning to redesign work around AI. My research explores how departments can deconstruct manual workflows into constituent tasks and rebuild them as AI-enabled systems with human-in-the-loop review and decision checkpoints.

## Background

Before returning to academia, I built my career in corporate communications and management consulting.

I rose to Executive Vice President at Edelman and served as a senior advisor to Fortune 500 C-suite leaders across technology, healthcare, biopharma, and financial services.

At Edelman, I founded the firm’s Behavioral Science capability within the Business Transformation practice and applied choice architecture and implementation science to rebuild enterprise workflows during large-scale change initiatives.
